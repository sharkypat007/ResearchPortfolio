I am Sarthak Patnaik, an alumnus of the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB) , where I earned my Master of Technology degree in Material Science. I graduated as the topper of my specialization and second in my department. My IITB research, under the guidance of Prof. Sumit Saxena , focused on designing sustainable recycling routes for efficient lithium recovery and direct recycling of end-of-life lithium iron phosphate (LFP) batteries. This research led to notable Publications, Patents, and acceptance in an International Conference.

I hold a Bachelor of Engineering in Metallurgical Engineering and Material Science from the Indian Institute of Technology Bhubaneswar (IITBBS). During my tenure at IITBBS, I researched under the guidance of Prof.Soobhankar Pati , designing hydrometallurgical routes for direct recycling of End-of-Life Li-ion batteries. This research led to a first-place finish in a National Hackathon, attending a National Workshop, as well as a notable Publication and a Patent.

Beyond academia, my concern for the environment has led me to actively contribute in an entrepreneurial setting at Cellark , where I have worked with various technologies in the domain of sustainable engineering. My knack for out-of-the-box solutions resulted in my representing Cellark at Together 2021, a venture contest organized by the Government of India and the Schulich School of Business Canada.

Beyond research, I am a globetrotter passionate about wildlife photography and an internationally-rated quizzer, always on the lookout for mind-bending facts and trivia. Explore my research and join my innovation journey.

Research Themes
Research Projects
Recycling of Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) Batteries
  • • Developed an efficient and cost-effective microwave assisted delithiation processfor LFP batteries
  • • Enabled high yield lithium and iron phosphate recovery and showed potential for industrial scalability
  • • Liquid state Lithiation routes were developed enabling regeneration of LiFePO4 from spent LFP
  • • Liquid state Sodiation routes were developed enabling regeneration of NaFePO4 from spent LFP
Novel Process for Recycling of Li-ion Batteries
  • • Conceptualized and further Lab Validated a Novel Route for Direct Recycling of Li-ion batteries
  • • Aspects of Hydro Metallurgy, Pyro Metallurgy and Preprocessing were evaluated for developed process
  • • Solid state relithiation studies were carried out by heating a lithium source in a reducing atmosphere
  • • Thermodynamic model and basic floor plan desing for the developed process
Recycling of End of Life Solar Panels
  • • Worked on establishing routes for End of Life Photo Voltaic (PV) Module Recycling
  • • Critical Material Recovery (Glass, Si-Wafer, Connecting Nodes) using hydrometallurgical routes
  • • EOL PV Modules being explored as a source of Si for potential electrodes for high-capacity LiBs
Development of Electro-less Deposition Techniques for Platinum Catalysts
  • • Designed Electro-less deposition techniques for platinum deposition on gold nano-particles
  • • The process showed potential for development of Fuel Cell catalysts
Corrosion Studies of the Konark Sun Temple's Iron Beams
  • • Working on investigating the Integrity of the Iron Beams at Konark Sun Temple
  • • Carried out electrochemical studies to evaluate corrosion behavior of 13th century Iron beams
  • • Project aims to gain insights into archeological corrosion enabling protection of monuments
